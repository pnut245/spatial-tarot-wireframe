# 10-minute usability test script (wireframe)

## Setup (1 min)

- Ask participant: tarot experience level (none / casual / frequent reader).
- Ask device: laptop + mouse / trackpad / phone.
- Tell them: “This is a wireframe. Treat it like a prototype: talk out loud; you can’t break it.”

## Task 1: Start + question (1 min)

1. Click **Start**.
2. Enter a question/intention.

**Ask:** “In one sentence, what do you think this tool is for?”

## Task 2: Draw + place (3 min)

1. Click **Draw 3** twice (6 cards total).
2. Place cards into slots in different planes.

**Observe:**

- Can they place a card without instruction (drag vs click-to-place)?
- Do they understand planes: Understory vs Surface vs Horizon?

## Task 3: Inspect (2 min)

1. Select a placed card.
2. Click **Flip** and **Reverse** once each.

**Ask:** “Is ‘Inspect’ at a comfortable reading distance? What feels missing for interpretation?”

## Task 4: Correspondences (2 min)

1. Hover over slot **B** (any plane).
2. Ask them to explain what the highlight across planes means.

**Ask:** “Would you actually use correspondences? Why/why not?”

## Task 5: Notes + export (2 min)

1. Write a note for the selected card and save it.
2. Click **Export JSON** and download it.

**Ask:** “If you were sharing this reading, what would you want the other person to see?”

## Metrics to capture

- Time-to-first-successful placement
- Misclicks / “where am I?” confusion moments
- Self-reported cognitive load (1–7) and clarity (1–7)
- Interpretation confidence (1–7)
- Biggest missing feature (one sentence)

## Success criteria (for deciding next build step)

- ≥80% of participants understand the planes without coaching
- ≥80% can place cards using at least one input method
- “Correspondence highlight” is understood by most participants (or you revise the metaphor/labels)
